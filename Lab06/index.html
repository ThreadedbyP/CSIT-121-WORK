Website Evaluation: Towson University

Website URL

Towson University/

Target Audience

The Towson University website is primarily designed for prospective and current students, faculty, staff, and alumni. It also serves parents, community members, and those seeking information about Towson's programs and events.

Site Organization

The website is structured with a clear navigation bar at the top that includes links to essential areas such as "Academics," "Admissions," and "Campus Life." Each section has well-organized subpages, making it easy for users to find the information they need.

CRAP Design Principles

The Towson University website incorporates the CRAP principles effectively:

  • Contrast: The use of bold black and yellow text on a white background creates strong contrast, making text readable and visually appealing.
  • Repetition: The consistent use of the university's branding colors and fonts throughout the site creates a cohesive look and feel.
  • Alignment: All content is neatly aligned, with clear structure for headings, images, and text blocks. Important information is centered and easy to follow.
  • Proximity: Related information, such as program descriptions and links to apply, are placed together, improving readability and usability.

Accessibility Audit Score

Using the Accessibility Checker, the Towson University website scored 71/100. The site is generally accessible with proper heading structures,it passed 25 audits but there are critical issues that needs fixing such as; alt text for images, and clear labels for forms.

Site Effectiveness

The site effectively helps users navigate the university's offerings, from academic programs to campus resources. The information is well-organized, and key actions like applying or requesting information are clearly accessible.

Site Efficiency

Users can quickly access important sections like admissions, course catalogs, or student services. The search functionality and clear navigation make it easy to perform tasks efficiently without unnecessary steps.

Site Engagement

The website is visually appealing and appropriate for an academic institution. The consistent use of the university's branding and images of students and faculty creates a welcoming and professional atmosphere.

Recommendation for Improvement

One area for improvement is the contrast in some of the action buttons like "Apply Now" or "Contact Us", which could be enhanced for better visibility, especially for users with visual impairments.
